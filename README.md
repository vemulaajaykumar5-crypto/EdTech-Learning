# 🎓 Cracking the "Over-Engagement Trap": How Product Analytics Uncovered a Hidden EdTech Churn Problem

Recently, I completed an end-to-end Product Analytics case study using student engagement data from an online learning platform.

## 🚨 The Business Problem

Most teams assume students drop out because they aren't engaged enough.

My analysis revealed something unexpected:

* High-risk students had **higher course progress (53.2%)**
* High-risk students had **higher quiz scores (71.0%)**
* Yet they were still more likely to churn

This contradicted the traditional assumption that low-performing students are the primary dropout risk.

## 🔍 Key Insight

After analyzing engagement patterns across 2,800 students using Python, SQL, and Power BI, I found evidence of an "Over-Engagement Trap."

Many high-performing learners were progressing rapidly through content but interacting very little with their peers.

These students often studied in isolation and lacked support when they encountered difficult concepts or assignments.

The result:

➡️ Cognitive burnout
➡️ Reduced community participation
➡️ Increased dropout risk

## 💡 Product Recommendation: Co-Learn Micro-Communities

To address isolation-driven churn, I designed a product feature called **Co-Learn**.

Instead of requiring students to leave their learning experience and search through a separate forum, the platform would automatically create contextual discussion groups for students working on the same module.

**Current Experience**

Video Lesson → Separate Forum → Low Participation → Dropout

**Proposed Experience**

Video Lesson + Live Module-Based Discussion Sidebar → Real-Time Support → Higher Retention

## 🧪 A/B Testing Framework

Hypothesis:

Students who receive contextual peer support will show higher engagement and lower churn.

Experiment Design:

* 50/50 Control vs Treatment split
* 30-day observation window
* Focus on Data Science and Design learners

Success Metrics:

* ≥5% reduction in high-risk churn
* ≥25% increase in platform interactions
* Improved course completion rates
